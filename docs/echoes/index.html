Why it exists

Not a lecture.
Something you step into.

I grew up in a small mountain town in the province of Caserta, southern Italy — the kind of place where everyone knows everyone, and that isn’t always a gift.

Without a way to get around, weekends belonged to other people’s groups, private jokes, and invitations I never received. Isolation wasn’t dramatic. It was quiet. And it followed me online.

What hurt most wasn’t the words. It was the silence — discovering that people you trusted talked about you, and that no one said stop.

I built Echoes because I needed it when I was a kid. Not a manifesto. An experience that places you on the other side of the screen — so you might pause before laughing, sharing, or staying silent.

The experience

You’re in the chat. Then you choose.

Echoes is an immersive narrative about exclusion and bystander silence — grounded in real research, designed to be heard as much as read.

  1. 01

    Enter a group chat

    A night out happened. Your friend wasn’t there. The morning after, everyone is talking — and Leo can read every message.

  2. 02

    Make three decisions

    Defend the person being left out, or keep scrolling. There are no “correct” answers — only the ones you make.

  3. 03

    See inside their mind

    After the chat, you enter the perspective of the person who was reading. Doubt. Shame. The weight of being unseen.

  4. 04

    Meet the data

    What you lived isn’t fiction for fiction’s sake. It’s rooted in research: too many young people have been here — and too few were ever asked how it felt.

“If this app makes even one person stop — before laughing, before sharing, before staying silent — then the kid who couldn’t make it to his friends’ houses built something that traveled farther than he ever could.”

— Marcello Guida
